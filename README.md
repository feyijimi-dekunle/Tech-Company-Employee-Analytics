# Tech-Company-Employee-Analytics
This repository contains an Excel-based analysis of a technology company’s employee dataset, focusing on workforce distribution, salary trends, experience levels, and age demographics. The project uses pivot tables, charts, and dashboards to generate clear business insights and support data-driven decision-making.

The analysis demonstrates practical data cleaning, pivot table analysis, and data visualization skills suitable for academic and portfolio purposes.


## Dataset Description
The dataset consists of 1,000 employee records from a technology company and includes the following attributes:
- Department  
- Gender  
- Age  
- Years of Experience  
- Salary  

The dataset supports descriptive workforce and compensation analysis.

---

## Analytical Questions Addressed
This project answers the following five business-focused questions:

1. How is the workforce distributed across departments and gender?  
2. Which departments have the highest and lowest average salaries?  
3. How does salary vary across different experience groups?  
4. What is the age distribution of employees across the company?  
5. Which department has the most experienced employees on average?  

---

## Tools and Techniques Used
- Microsoft Excel  
- Data Cleaning and Preparation  
- Helper Columns (Age Groups and Experience Groups)  
- Pivot Tables  
- Pivot Charts  
- Dashboard Design  

---

## Summary of Insights
- Workforce distribution across departments and gender shows a relatively balanced structure.
- Average salary levels differ across departments, indicating role-based compensation patterns.
- Salary generally increases as years of experience increase.
- The company workforce is largely made up of early to mid-career employees.
- Certain departments have higher average experience levels compared to others.
